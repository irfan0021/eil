Cargo UAV Features:

EIL Arms And Ammunition Ltd was incorporated in 2025 for the manufacture of Défense Equipment.

The Cargo UAV is designed to carry nominal payloads with ample margin for packaging and attachments. It operates at medium altitudes, typically between 1,000 and 6,000 meters above sea level, ensuring efficient cruise performance and smooth integration within existing airspace. Featuring fixed-wing VTOL airframe options, it offers a perfect balance between endurance and runway independence. The cruise speed is optimized for fuel and energy efficiency while maintaining timely delivery, providing a practical trade-off between speed and range.

The Cargo UAV is designed to carry nominal payloads with ample margin for packaging and attachments. It operates at medium altitudes, typically between 1,000 and 6,000 meters above sea level, ensuring efficient cruise performance and smooth integration within existing airspace. Featuring fixed-wing VTOL airframe options, it offers a perfect balance between endurance and runway independence. The cruise speed is optimized for fuel and energy efficiency while maintaining timely delivery, providing a practical trade-off between speed and range.

Its payload bay includes modular mounting options and standardized tie-down points to accommodate diverse cargo types. A quick-release cargo door or winch system enables precise delivery or the handling of suspended loads. The UAV’s structural design ensures stability and safety even when managing uneven or shifting cargo, supported by redundant flight controls and actuators that enhance reliability.

The system supports multiple propulsion options—electric, hybrid, or internal combustion engines—allowing flexibility to meet range and turnaround needs. With sufficient fuel or energy capacity, it is capable of covering mission ranges with reserves for contingencies. Advanced avionics enable autonomous waypoint navigation and dynamic re-tasking, while robust GNSS systems with multi-constellation and inertial aiding ensure navigation resilience.

Safety features include detect-and-avoid or ADS-B interoperability for airspace compliance, alongside a secure, encrypted command-and-control datalink with redundant telemetry. Designed for BVLOS (Beyond Visual Line of Sight) operations, it incorporates certified ground-control procedures and remote pilot interfaces. Real-time health monitoring covers propulsion, airframe, power systems, and payload conditions, supported by environmental controls like shock mounting, vibration isolation, and temperature or humidity regulation.

Cargo weight and center-of-gravity sensors provide both pre-flight and in-flight monitoring. Integrated weather sensing assists in avoiding adverse conditions such as strong winds, precipitation, or icing. The UAV also minimizes logistical footprint through modular batteries or fuel pods, field-swappable components, and maintenance-friendly access points for efficient turnaround and condition-based servicing.

To ensure compliance and reliability, the platform includes certification pathways for civil aviation, BVLOS operations, and airworthiness regulations. Data recording and audit logs support flight tracking, payload custody, and incident investigation. Cybersecurity measures protect mission planning, telemetry, and payload interfaces, while interoperability with fleet management and logistics systems allows seamless scheduling, manifest control, and tracking.

For safety and community integration, the Cargo UAV incorporates emergency abort and safe-landing protocols, controlled payload jettison options, and low-noise, low-emission designs suitable for populated areas. Built for scalability, it supports increased payload or range through modular platform variants and adaptable add-ons.
